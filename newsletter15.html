State of AI Ethics report 2020

We released our State of AI Ethics June 2020 report which captured the most impactful and meaningful research and development from across the world and compiled them into a single source.

Research Summaries

Classical Ethics in A/IS.

The ethical implications of autonomous and intelligent systems (A/IS) are, by now, notably numerous and complex.

The topics of inquiry covered in the paper are wide, and each is given between two and four pages of background and subsequent recommendations.

Research Summaries

Aligning Super Human AI with Human Behavior: Chess as a Model System

AI systems typically approach problems and decision making differently than the way people do.

The researchers in this study created a new model that explores human chess players’ behavior at a move-by-move level and the development of chess algorithms that match the human move-level behavior.

Research Summaries

Toward Fairness in AI for People with Disabilities: A Research Roadmap

Although AI can be extremely beneficial to these populations (the paper provides several examples of such benefits),

there is a risk of these systems not working properly for PWD or even discriminating against them.

This paper is an effort towards identifying how inclusion issues for PWD may impact AI.

From the Learning Communities

Countering Information Influence Activities: The State of the Art

The report frames the defense challenge in three parts:

Legitimacy: They are illegitimate attempts to change opinions in democratic states

Intention: They are conducted to benefit foreign powers (state, non-state, or proxy)

Ambiguity: They are conducted in a gray zone of hybrid threat between peace and war

Article Summaries

Climate Denial Spreads on Facebook as Scientists Face Restrictions

Facebook has weakened protections against the spread of climate change related disinformation

Facebook overruled the decision provided by an independent fact-checking body.

A leading climate scientist’s posts were branded as political thus requiring her to provide more private information before she will be allowed to promote and publish posts

Article Summaries

Speech Recognition Tech Is Yet Another Example of Bias

In human speech, we make accommodations for each other on a daily basis to be able to negotiate and navigate different accents and dialects.

The study linked in the article points to how black people in the US face a disproportionate burden of such speech-related discrimination that imposes an unnecessary choice for them to either abandon their identity or abandon the use of those devices.

Article Summaries

Researchers identify dozens of words that accidentally trigger Amazon Echo speakers

LeakyPick, an approach that was used to check accidental triggering of smart voice assistants to start capturing audio snippets even when they weren’t invoked.

There were demonstrable and repeatable instances where the devices woke up and recorded conversations when they weren’t invoked using the actual wakeword.

Article Summaries

An experiment in end-of-life care: Tapping AI’s cold calculus to nudge the most human of conversations

Doctors are put in awkward positions bringing up the most intimate and sensitive thing up to a patient based on the recommendation of a machine.

Another problem to be highlighted is a potential over-reliance on the system for making these decisions and reducing the autonomy that doctors would have.

Article Summaries

Don’t ask if artificial intelligence is good or fair, ask how it shifts power

When the field of AI believes it is neutral, it both fails to notice biased data and builds systems that sanctify the status quo and advance the interests of the powerful.

What is needed is a field that exposes and critiques systems that concentrate power, while co-creating new systems with impacted communities: AI by and for the people.

Article Summaries

Challenges of Comparing Human and Machine Perception

The first pitfall is how humans are quick to conclude machines have learned human-like concepts.

The second pitfall is that it is hard to draw conclusions about the generalizing beyond tested architectures and training procedures.

The final pitfall is finding strong parallels in the experimental setups to compare humans and machines.

This paper seeks to investigate how we might foster an inclusive, foresight-informed responsible AI governance framework.
